### 1. Overall Aim
The figure should read as a high-level scientific architecture for a major research proposal, not as a software product screenshot, engineering dashboard, or vendor solution diagram. The visual objective is conceptual clarity, intellectual hierarchy, and publication-grade restraint.

### 2. Visual Tone
- Use a calm, rigorous, macro-scale visual tone.
- Prefer white or warm ivory backgrounds with generous whitespace.
- Use restrained blue-gray, muted teal, and pale sand accents.
- Keep saturation low; use stronger contrast only for the most important semantic emphasis.
- Avoid neon colors, glossy enterprise aesthetics, black backgrounds, or poster-like visual aggression.

### 3. Composition Principles
- Organize the figure into a few large conceptual regions rather than many tiny modules.
- Preserve a strong hierarchy: substrate, control/evidence, orchestration, closed-loop interaction.
- Emphasize flow and structure using a small number of clear arrows.
- Keep the eye moving smoothly across the figure; do not create cluttered dense tangles.
- Use consistent spacing, alignment, line weight, and corner radius.

### 4. Semantic Style
- Favor conceptual symbols and abstract scientific motifs over UI widgets.
- Avoid dashboard panels, browser windows, excessive badges, or operational console metaphors.
- Avoid cartoon characters, playful mascots, and decorative robots.
- Use only short labels. Do not render long sentences inside the figure.
- The figure should feel like a scientific explanatory architecture, not a workflow poster.

### 5. Typography and Labels
- Use a small number of concise labels with clean, high-legibility typography.
- Prefer architectural nouns and stage names, not procedural instruction text.
- Avoid verbose captions inside the image. The surrounding paper or proposal will carry the prose.

### 6. Common Failure Modes to Avoid
- Too many small boxes.
- Strong software engineering or product-demo flavor.
- Excessive icon mixing.
- Repeated or redundant labels.
- Overly literal data-center/cloud clipart.
- Dense black outlines and flat PowerPoint styling.

### 7. Desired Effect
The final image should feel precise, quiet, authoritative, and proposal-ready. A reviewer should perceive it as a distilled scientific architecture that supports a major research agenda.
